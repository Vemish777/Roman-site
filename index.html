ABOUT ME
  • Hi, I’m Roman, a tattoo artist with 7 years of experience. I specialize in microrealism — I value precision, depth of detail, and powerful emotional impact, even in the smallest pieces. For me, every tattoo is more than just ink on skin; it’s a personal story, captured in lines and shadows.
  • Three years ago, I moved from Ukraine to Germany, escaping the war. Here, far from home, tattooing became more than just my profession — it became my way of communicating with the world, quietly, sincerely, and in my own language. That journey made my art deeper, more honest — and maybe a little melancholic, but all the more real.
  • Before becoming a tattoo artist, I studied architecture — something that often surprises my clients. But in truth, architecture taught me composition, working with form, and a deep respect for detail — all of which I now bring into my tattoo work. I also come from a creative family: my father is a sculptor, so I guess you could say that art runs in my blood.
  • If you’re looking for a tattoo with soul, precision, and a meaningful story — you’re in the right place. I’d be happy to create something truly special for you.

INFO

Is it safe to get a tattoo?
Yes, it is completely safe for most people. Tattoos are safe if the tattoo artist strictly follows safety precautions and sanitary rules. Compliance with sanitary regulations and client safety is a priority for the tattoo artist. The artist ensures the highest possible level of sterilization of tattoo tools, uses disposable sterile needles, and works only with certified pigments.
Are there any special requirements for a tattoo?

You must be at least 18 years old to get a tattoo in accordance with the law and health regulations. To get a tattoo, you must present a birth certificate or a valid photo ID (such as a passport or driver’s license).

Is it painful?
Pain varies by individual and placement, but most clients tolerate it well.

Designs
  • I almost always use and create my own designs based on your ideas, but sometimes we can take something from the internet. Most often, this applies to small tattoos or something that doesn't need any changes (for example, if you want the logo of your favorite rock band).

  • Design creation is a very individual and detailed process, so I usually prepare only the base of the design (about 70% of the final version), and we finish the rest together on the day of the session before starting the work. This is much easier due to many factors like size/placement or if you want to add words/dates that have a personal meaning to you. That's why it's much easier for me to explain and do all this with you in person

  • Sometimes I send you the full design in advance, but that's rare and usually only for large projects (backs, legs, etc.)
    • Before getting tattoo
  • A tattoo is work with your skin,so please follow these simple rules to make it easier for me to work and to ensure the best possible final result

  • You should not have any skin damage (including sunburn) for a week before your tattoo.
  • For a week before your tattoo, please moisturize the area where the tattoo will be (especially if you have dry skin!).
  • Avoid tanning beds and hair removal on the tattoo area for three days before your appointment.
  • Do not drink alcohol or take blood-affecting medication the day before your session.
  • On the day of the session, avoid strong coffee/tea/energy drinks (marijuana is also not recommended).
  • Get a good night's sleep and eat well before your session - this helps reduce pain.
  • Bring comfortable clothing (you can change at the studio) and bring a snack if we're planning a long session.

  • Also, a personal request from me - please wear neutral-colored clothes (black, white, beige), as this helps me take high-quality photos of the tattoo that I can later send to you.
    • Aftercare
  • The beauty of your tattoo depends only 50% on me - the other 50% is how you take care of it during healing. Here are the instructions:

  • After I apply the second skin, avoid long and hot showers or baths (quick and normal showers are fine).
  • In the next few days, small bubbles may appear under the second skin or a bit of ink may collect - this is completely normal, please don't touch them.
  • On the fifth day after the session, you can remove the second skin - to make it easier, hold the tattoo under warm water for a few minutes and gently peel it off.
  • In the first few days after removal, do not use a shower sponge on the tattooed area - wash it gently with your hands.
  • Do not scratch or peel off flaking skin - it will come off on its own within a few days.
  • After removing the second skin, be sure to moisturize your tattoo with a thin layer of cream 1-2 times a day to help your skin recover.
  • Avoid sweating, sports, and alcohol for one week. Avoid saunas, pools, tanning beds, the sea, and sun exposure for 3 weeks after the session.
